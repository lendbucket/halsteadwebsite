# Dealer Program — Frequently Asked Questions

Detailed answers to common questions from prospective Halstead dealers.

---

## The basics

### What is Halstead Security?
Halstead Security is a national security brand operated through a network of independent, locally-owned dealers. Each dealer holds an exclusive territory, runs their own state-licensed alarm business, and operates on Halstead's software platform with Halstead's brand and back-office support.

### Is Halstead a franchise?
No. Halstead is structured as a software platform with a trademark license, not a franchise. This is a deliberate choice — it means more flexibility for dealers, less regulatory burden for both parties, and faster path to launch. Dealers operate as independent businesses under a license agreement.

### How does Halstead make money?
Three ways: (1) the one-time territory fee from each dealer, (2) a monthly platform fee from each dealer, and (3) a 30% share of customer subscription revenue. We make money when our dealers make money — our incentives are aligned.

### What does a dealer actually do?
Dealers handle the local relationships: marketing, sales, installation, customer support, and ongoing service. Halstead handles everything else: software, billing, hardware sourcing, brand, training, compliance tools.

## The $15,000 founding dealer fee

### Why $15,000?
We benchmarked against existing dealer programs. ADT Authorized Dealer requires $50,000+ committed. Brinks requires $25,000-$100,000. Vivint requires $30,000-$50,000. Independent alarm company launches cost $30,000-$75,000 with no brand recognition. $15,000 for everything Halstead provides is intentionally priced below the competition for our founding cohort.

### What's included in the $15,000?
- Exclusive geographic territory (50K-150K population)
- Halstead brand license
- Full software platform
- State licensing wizard
- 5-day intensive training and certification
- Marketing kit (templates, signs, ads, collateral)
- 90-day onboarding support
- 25 free customer setups (worth $2,475)
- 5 starter inventory kits at platform cost (worth $2,000)
- Founding rate locked for 24 months

### What's not included?
- State licensing fees ($400-$1,500 depending on state)
- Surety bond ($500-$2,000 annual)
- Insurance ($1,500-$3,500 annual)
- LLC formation ($300-$500)
- Background check ($50-$150)
- Your own initial inventory beyond the 5 starter kits
- Vehicle, signage materials, business expenses

### Is the $15,000 refundable?
Refundable in full if Halstead does not approve your application or if Halstead changes the program structure within 30 days of your payment. Non-refundable after training begins.

### Can I finance the $15,000?
Yes. Reyna Title (a sister company) offers founding dealer financing for qualified applicants — typically 12 to 24 month terms at competitive rates. Apply with your dealer application.

### What about taxes on the $15,000?
The fee is generally a capital expense for your business — consult your CPA on the specifics for your state and entity structure.

## Territory & exclusivity

### How is a territory defined?
By ZIP codes. Each territory is a defined cluster of contiguous ZIP codes covering 50,000 to 150,000 in population. You can review available territories on the dealer application.

### What does "exclusive" mean?
No other Halstead dealer can sell, install, or monitor in your territory. Halstead corporate doesn't compete with you in your territory either. The brand is yours alone within your zone.

### What if a customer in my territory already had Halstead through someone else?
Pre-existing customers transfer to you on territory activation if they were initially served by a previous dealer who exited. National marketing leads in your zone route to you.

### Can I expand my territory later?
Yes. After 12 months of meeting performance thresholds, you can apply to expand into adjacent territories at the standard rate ($25,000) or multi-territory rate ($10,000 each, up to 5 total).

### What happens if I'm not performing?
We have a "use it or lose it" clause. Specifically:
- 90-day check-in (informal)
- 6-month performance review
- 12-month formal review against minimum thresholds
- 24 months below threshold = territory forfeiture with right of first refusal to a neighboring dealer

Minimums are realistic for a dealer doing the work — the goal is to ensure customers in every territory are well-served, not to take territories back.

## State licensing

### Do I need a state alarm license?
Yes. Every state requires some form of license to sell, install, or monitor alarm systems. Halstead's licensing wizard walks you through your state's specific requirements.

### What if I don't have alarm industry experience?
Some states require a "qualifying party" with documented industry experience. If you don't have it personally, you can hire a qualifying party as a W-2 employee. Halstead can help you find one in most markets.

### How long does state licensing take?
Texas: 6-8 weeks. Most other states: 6-12 weeks. We recommend starting the licensing process in parallel with your training.

### What does state licensing cost?
Varies by state. Roughly $400-$1,500 in fees, plus surety bond ($500-$2,000), plus insurance ($1,500-$3,500). Halstead's licensing wizard breaks this down by state.

### Can Halstead provide my state license?
No. Each dealer must hold their own state license. This is both a legal requirement and a structural protection — your business has its own license that's not dependent on Halstead's continued operation.

### What if my state changes its licensing rules?
Halstead's wizard is updated as states change requirements. We notify all dealers in affected states immediately when we learn of a change.

## Training & operations

### What does the 5-day training cover?
- Day 1: Product knowledge — every camera, sensor, and hub we sell
- Day 2: Sales — qualifying leads, pricing, closing, objection handling
- Day 3: Installation — mobile workflow, customer handoff, troubleshooting
- Day 4: Customer service and ongoing support
- Day 5: Compliance, contracts, recording disclosure laws

### Where does training happen?
Currently online via video sessions, with hands-on hardware practice using your starter kits. We're planning in-person training events in 2026.

### What's the certification?
A series of quizzes (one per training day) that you must pass at 80% or better. Passing the certifications unlocks your ability to enable customer billing.

### Do I need to hire installers?
For your first 5-10 customers, you can install yourself. Most dealers hire 1-2 installers per 100 customers as they scale. Some dealers run their territory part-time with one installer; others build out a full crew.

### How do customer installs actually work?
Customer signs up through your website (which Halstead provides) or through your direct sales. They're billed for hardware up front, then the recurring subscription. Hardware drop-ships from Halstead's central warehouse to the customer. You schedule the install. On install day, you (or your installer) opens the Halstead mobile app, follows the step-by-step install workflow, scans QR codes to pair devices, captures customer signature, and closes the install ticket.

### What if a customer has problems?
Customer first contacts you (their local Halstead dealer). You troubleshoot — most issues are simple wifi, power, or position fixes. If you can't resolve it, you escalate to Halstead's platform-tier support.

## Money & economics

### What does a dealer actually earn?
Conservative example at 50 customers (Year 1):
- Subscription share: $1,050/month
- Hardware margin: $1,000/month
- Less platform fee: -$499/month
- **Net: $1,550/month, $18,600/year**

ROI on $15,000 fee: ~10 months.

At 200 customers (Year 3):
- Subscription share: $4,200/month
- Hardware margin: $3,500/month
- Less platform fee: -$499/month
- **Net: $7,200/month, $86,400/year**

These projections are reasonable but not guaranteed.

### What's the platform fee for?
The $499/month covers software access, hosting, customer-facing portal, mobile app, ongoing platform development, central marketing, and platform-tier support.

### What's the 30% subscription split for?
Customer subscriptions are billed to Halstead's platform via Stripe. Stripe takes its merchant fee. Halstead retains 30% (covering platform fee, brand royalty, central station relationships, software development). 70% flows to the dealer's Stripe Connect account.

### What's hardware margin?
You buy hardware from Halstead at wholesale, sell to customers at retail. Wholesale to retail markup is typically 30-50% depending on kit. You keep 100% of that margin (Halstead's margin is in the wholesale price you pay).

### When do I get paid?
Customer subscription payouts arrive in your Stripe Connect account on Stripe's standard schedule (typically 2-day rolling). Hardware payments are immediate at install. Halstead's platform fee is auto-debited monthly.

### What about taxes?
You're responsible for your own business taxes. Halstead provides a 1099-K via Stripe for your subscription revenue and order data export for hardware.

## Risks & questions

### What if Halstead the company fails?
The dealer agreement includes provisions for platform discontinuance. Specifically:
- Your customer book stays yours
- Equipment continues to function as standalone cameras
- Halstead would help you migrate to an alternative platform
- Your territory exclusivity ends, but your business doesn't

### What if I want out?
After 24 months you can transfer your territory to a qualified buyer. Halstead has right of first refusal at fair market value. Most established territories sell at 3-5x annual gross profit.

### What if I just want to walk away?
You can terminate the dealer agreement with 60 days notice (after the first year). Customers transfer to a neighboring dealer or to Halstead corporate. No refund of the territory fee.

### What if Halstead grows really fast and I get diluted?
Founding dealers (first 25) get rate locks for 24 months and right of first refusal on adjacent territory expansions. Your founding rate isn't subject to dilution from later cohorts.

### What if a competitor offers me something better?
We hope you stay because Halstead delivers. But the dealer agreement is not exclusive — you're not prevented from running other businesses or other product lines. (You are prevented from operating a competing security brand in your Halstead territory while you're a Halstead dealer.)

### What about liability if something goes wrong at a customer site?
Each party indemnifies the other appropriately. Dealer carries general liability ($1M minimum) and E&O insurance. Halstead carries platform-level coverage. The dealer agreement spells out specific indemnification terms.
