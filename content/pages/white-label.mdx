# Run a security business under your own brand.

Halstead's white-label platform lets established security companies, smart home installers, and adjacent service providers operate under their own brand using Halstead's full platform, hardware sourcing, and back-office support.

This is for operators who want to scale a real security business without building the software, sourcing the hardware, or figuring out the regulatory layer themselves.

## Who white-label is for

### Existing alarm companies
You have customers, installers, and a state license, but your software is a Frankenstein of email, spreadsheets, and a 2008-era CRM. Halstead's platform replaces all of that and lets you compete with national brands on technology while keeping your local relationships.

### Adjacent home services brands
You're a successful HVAC, plumbing, or electrical company in a regional market. Adding a security line under your existing brand is a natural extension. Customers already trust you. Halstead provides the platform and product so you don't have to build them.

### Property management groups
You manage thousands of doors. Halstead's white-label platform lets you offer security as a value-add to your tenants under your own brand, with consolidated billing and centralized portal access.

### Real estate brokerages
You close hundreds of homes a year. Bundle Halstead-powered security under your brokerage's brand as a closing gift or add-on service. Recurring revenue stream that complements your transaction-based business.

## How white-label is different from being a dealer

| Aspect | Dealer | White-Label |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Brand | Halstead | Your own |
| Territory | Exclusive geographic | Open / multi-region |
| Entry cost | $15,000 founding | $5,000-$15,000 setup |
| Monthly fee | $499 platform | $1,500-$3,000 |
| Ideal for | Aspiring entrepreneurs | Established operators |
| Brand recognition value | Halstead-built | Customer's own |

If you're starting fresh and want a national brand to ride on, become a dealer. If you have an established business and want to scale it on Halstead's infrastructure, go white-label.

White-label partnerships require a more involved evaluation than dealer applications. We're selective about who runs operations under their own brand on our infrastructure.

3. **Reference checks** — both directions (you check us, we check you)
4. **Commercial discussion** — pricing, scope, custom requirements
5. **Contract** — master services agreement plus statement of work
6. **Onboarding** — typically 30-60 days to launch
