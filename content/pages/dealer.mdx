You buy hardware from Halstead at wholesale, sell to customers at retail. **20-50% margin per kit.** Drop-shipped from our central warehouse, no inventory required.

Annual maintenance visits, battery replacements, equipment upgrades — **dealer keeps 100%.**

4. Complete the state alarm licensing process (Halstead's wizard guides you)

6. Carry the appropriate state surety bond
7. Complete and pass Halstead's 5-day training and certification
8. Commit to the brand standards in your territory
9. Hit minimum performance thresholds (or risk territory forfeiture after 24 months)

## Frequently asked

**Is this a franchise?**

No. Halstead is structured as a software platform with a trademark license, not a franchise. You operate as an independent business under a license agreement. This means more flexibility for you, less regulatory burden for both of us, and a faster path to launch.

**What if I don't have alarm industry experience?**

That's fine for many states. Some states require a "qualifying party" with specific industry experience — Halstead can help you identify and hire one. Others require a state exam that we'll prepare you for. The licensing wizard walks you through state-specific requirements.

**Do I need to hire installers?**

Eventually, yes. For your first 5-10 customers you can install yourself. As you scale, most dealers hire 1-2 installers per 100 customers. Some dealers run their territory part-time with one installer; others build a team.

**What if I want to operate in multiple states?**

You can hold up to 5 territories. Each additional territory is $10,000. You'd need a separate state license in each operating state.

**Can I sell my territory later?**

Yes. After 24 months, territories are transferable. Halstead has right of first refusal at fair market value. Most established territories sell at 3-5x annual gross profit.

**What if Halstead the company fails?**

The dealer agreement includes provisions for platform discontinuance. Your customer book stays yours, equipment stays in customers' homes, and we'd help you migrate to an alternative platform. Your territory exclusivity ends, but your business doesn't.

**What's not negotiable?**

Brand standards. Customer experience requirements. The 30% subscription split. Minimum performance thresholds. Background and credit checks. State licensing.

**What is negotiable?**

Territory boundaries (within reason). Multi-territory pricing. Master dealer arrangements for established alarm operators with existing customer books. Co-op marketing levels.
